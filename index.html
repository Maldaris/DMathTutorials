Mathematical Induction

Induction is the proof of equality statements, usually involving Sigma Notation statements. Induction occurs in two steps, the base step, proving the initial equality, or "base" case, usually where n = 0 or n = 1. The Induction step, from which the technique gets its name, occurs when looking at the n = n+1 step. It's easiest to show directly, so let's get started.
